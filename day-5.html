Day 5

Incident Grouping and Noise Metrics

In Progress

Day 5 is building the analyst evidence layer around grouped alerts, reduced noise, lifecycle views, and executive-friendly SOC metrics.

Completed This Sprint

  • Alert queue filters added for search, severity, verdict, and analyst queue.
  • Case lifecycle split into board, intake, timeline, and closure sections.
  • Executive dashboard charts added for severity distribution, incident status, and seven-day triage trend.
  • Noise-reduction endpoint already exposes raw alert count, grouped duplicates, suppressed noise, analyst items, and reduction percentage.
  • Current triage logic returns correlation key, correlation count, related alert count, entity frequency, suppression decision, and suppression reason.
  • Persisted correlation groups added with representative alert, grouped alert IDs, first/last seen, max risk, max signal, average noise, and suppression reason.
  • AI Triage Workbench now includes a Correlation Groups tab for L1/L2 investigation evidence.
  • Local IOC watchlist added for lab-safe enrichment without sending IOCs to external providers.
  • Alert detail can run local IOC enrichment and show match count, severity, checked candidates, and source metadata.

Still Building

  • Promotion rule from repeated high-signal groups into incidents.
  • Threat-intel IOC lookup cache and triage detail enrichment.
  • External threat-intel adapters for VirusTotal, AbuseIPDB, OTX, and MISP.
  • Long-range metrics snapshots for 7 days, 15 days, 30 days, and 1 year.
  • Analyst feedback override that influences duplicate triage decisions.
