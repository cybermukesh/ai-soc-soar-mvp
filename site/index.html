Judge summary

What We Are Building

The product is an AI-assisted SOC/SOAR layer for analysts. It reads SIEM alerts, removes repeated noise, gives a concise attack analysis, groups related events into incidents, and starts response actions through n8n or Shuffle after analyst approval.

  • Built first on Wazuh because it is open-source and hackathon-friendly.
  • Designed with a normalized alert schema so other SIEMs can replace Wazuh later.
  • Focused on analyst productivity, not a generic chatbot interface.

Industry pain

Why It Matters

SOC teams face alert overload, repeated false positives, limited analyst time, and expensive enterprise automation platforms. Smaller teams often have detection tools but not enough people or budget to convert alerts into clean, prioritized response workflows.

  • Analysts waste time reading low-context raw alerts.
  • Escalation quality depends heavily on individual analyst experience.
  • Commercial AI SOC copilots are often costly and closed.

Token and cost controls

Cheap Cloud By Default

Do not fine-tune in MVP

Use prompt engineering, deterministic schema, and structured JSON before spending on model training.

Send compact context

Only normalized alert fields, severity, rule, asset, timeline, and minimal history go to the model.

Cache duplicate triage

Repeated alerts reuse the prior verdict unless the asset, severity, or evidence changes.

Escalate low confidence

Do not keep calling models for unclear cases. Mark for analyst review with the current evidence.

Use JSON-only output

Keep responses short, parseable, and audit-friendly for storage and dashboard rendering.

Reserve stronger models

Use larger models only for demo-critical summaries or high-severity incidents when required.
